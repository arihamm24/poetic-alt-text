Poetic Alt Text

This website was developed for the Fulton County Public Arts Future Lab to complement their ADA 36th Anniversary Celebration.

It is a digital adaptation and extension of the Alt Text as Poetry Workbook written by Shannon Finnegan and Bojana Coklyat as part of the “Alt Text as Poetry” Project.

Our goal is to provide a resource to teach best practice about alt text and re-orient alt text authorship as a creative and connective exercise in meaning making. That is, we hope to present alt text as a poetic form of expression that could be particularly useful for artists in several media.

Alt Text 101: What is alt text? Who is it for? How is it used?

Art and Alt Text as Poetry: How does alt text make meaning? How does it translate visual information? How can be used in creative spaces?

Interactive Exercises: We have adapted the four exercises featured in the original workbook into digital interactives. Because we adapted them for individual use, we still recommend referring to the workbook and completing the original exercises with a partner as intended.

References and more information: We are far from an authoritative source. Review our references, double check our information, and explore more on your own.
